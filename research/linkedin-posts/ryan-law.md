# Ryan Law — LinkedIn Post Research

Ryan Law is Director of Content Marketing at Ahrefs (formerly CMO at Animalz), and one of the most consistently cited voices on LinkedIn for AI content production, AI search/SEO, and content operations. The five posts below were selected for being high-signal, frequently referenced, and directly actionable for AI SEO and content-ops research, spanning his early predictions about generative AI through his current production systems.

---

## 1. Ahrefs Analyzes 1 Billion Data Points on AI Search Optimization

**Post Title / Topic:** AI search optimization research findings (11 internal Ahrefs studies)
**Date:** February 2026
**Post URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/thinkingslow_in-the-last-3-months-at-ahrefs-we-analyzed-activity-7424382429542486016--OIQ

**Summary:** Law summarized three months of Ahrefs research spanning 11 studies and over a billion data points on what actually drives visibility in AI search. The headline finding is that YouTube mentions are the single strongest predictor of AI visibility, outperforming Domain Rating and backlinks. He also reported that AI Mode and AI Overviews tend to reach the same conclusions for a given query but cite almost entirely different sources, and that content length has essentially no correlation with AI citations. He closed with data showing that freshness is a major factor: a large majority of pages cited by ChatGPT in list-style answers had been updated within the past year, and many within the past month.

**Key Insight:** Classic SEO ranking signals (page length, backlinks, Domain Rating) are weak predictors of AI citation; video presence and content freshness are far more influential for AI search visibility.

**Relevance to AI SEO Research:** This is primary-source, large-sample data on what correlates with AI Overview/AI Mode/ChatGPT citations, making it directly useful for benchmarking or challenging assumptions in AI SEO strategy.

## 2. Full Walkthrough of an AI Content Automation System (Built with Agent A)

**Post Title / Topic:** End-to-end AI content production pipeline
**Date:** 2026 (recent)
**Post URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/thinkingslow_heres-a-full-9-minute-walkthrough-of-my-activity-7464941601384128512-4-xC

**Summary:** Law shared a 9-minute video walking through the no-code AI content system he built using Ahrefs' AI agent, "Agent A." The system covers the full editorial lifecycle: keyword research, competitor and topic analysis, outlining, drafting, internal linking, basic image generation, formatting, and even post-publish content updates. It's powered by dozens of customizable "skill" files that encode his real editorial process and pull directly from Ahrefs product data, and he notes it has been used to publish and update numerous live blog articles. He also lists the specific failure patterns that make AI content underperform: relying only on a model's internal knowledge instead of external sourcing, missing links/images/first-person experience, and leaving detectable AI artifacts or un-"humanized" phrasing.

**Key Insight:** Production-grade AI content comes from a modular, repeatable system of editorial "skill files" rather than a single prompt, and most AI content underperforms because of missing external sourcing and detectable AI phrasing patterns, not because AI writing is inherently weak.

**Relevance to AI SEO Research:** Offers a concrete, reusable blueprint for an AI content production and editorial workflow pipeline, plus a checklist of failure modes to test for when evaluating AI-generated SEO content.

## 3. Launching Ahrefs AI Content Helper

**Post Title / Topic:** Product philosophy behind an AI-powered content optimization tool
**Date:** September 2024
**Post URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/thinkingslow_very-excited-about-this-ahrefs-ai-content-activity-7241458552798658560-n0v-

**Summary:** Announcing the beta launch of Ahrefs' AI Content Helper, Law explained why he intentionally avoided the keyword-density/content-score model used by most optimization tools, arguing those scores encourage keyword stuffing and reductive writing. Instead, the tool uses AI to identify the core topics that top-ranking pages actually cover for a given keyword and scores a draft (and competitors) against topical coverage in real time, including intent-specific analysis and AI-assisted metadata brainstorming. He frames the design goal as keeping the writer in control of creative decisions rather than handing that control to the tool.

**Key Insight:** AI content tooling should optimize for comprehensive topical coverage and preserve writer judgment, rather than mechanically scoring keyword density, which tends to produce generic, interchangeable content.

**Relevance to AI SEO Research:** Lays out a clear, contrarian product philosophy for AI-assisted SEO content tools, useful for evaluating or designing content-optimization software that avoids the keyword-stuffing failure mode.

## 4. 5 Reasons Why AI Content Is a Big Strategic Mistake

**Post Title / Topic:** Strategic risk of scaling AI-generated content
**Date:** July 2023
**Post URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/thinkingslow_contentmarketing-growth-seo-activity-7081620877682860032-xR1N

**Summary:** Law argued against treating mass AI content generation as a sound long-term growth strategy, characterizing it instead as short-term arbitrage rather than durable competitive advantage. His core concerns were that unmoderated AI publishing erodes brand trust, accelerates saturation of search results with low-differentiation content, introduces quality and accuracy risk without strong editorial oversight, and ultimately trades away the kind of distinctive content that drives sustainable organic growth. The post generated significant debate among content and SEO practitioners about where the line sits between efficient AI assistance and strategic overreach.

**Key Insight:** Scaling content volume with AI, without editorial rigor, sacrifices long-term brand and search equity for short-term output gains — it's an arbitrage play, not a strategy.

**Relevance to AI SEO Research:** Provides an important counterweight to volume-focused AI publishing approaches, framing the brand and SERP-saturation risks that AI SEO strategies need to account for.

## 5. The Future of SEO in a Post-ChatGPT World

**Post Title / Topic:** Strategic forecast for content marketing roles after generative AI
**Date:** April 2023
**Post URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/thinkingslow_chatgpt-seo-ai-activity-7051129939219083264-q2a5

**Summary:** In one of his most widely discussed posts (80+ comments), Law used a "negative visualization" exercise to game out worst-case scenarios for content marketing as generative AI matured — content becoming commoditized, willingness-to-pay for content collapsing, SEO returns shrinking, and the field flooding with AI-written material. His proposed response was to ride the early-adoption wave by becoming genuinely fluent in the new tools while that's still a differentiator, and to move personal and team value up the chain toward higher-leverage skills like data analysis, distribution, technical SEO, and editorial strategy rather than pure writing.

**Key Insight:** As AI commoditizes writing execution, durable career and competitive advantage in content marketing shifts toward research, distribution, technical SEO, and editorial judgment.

**Relevance to AI SEO Research:** An early but still-relevant strategic framework for how content and SEO teams should reposition skills and team structure as AI reshapes search and content economics.

## Overall Themes

A few ideas recur across all five posts. First, Law consistently treats editorial judgment and human oversight as the actual differentiator in AI-era content — the tools change, but quality still depends on sourcing, structure, and a strong editorial process layered on top of AI output, not the AI output alone. Second, he repeatedly argues that classic SEO heuristics (content length, keyword density, backlink volume) are losing predictive power, being replaced by signals like topical depth, freshness, and multimedia presence (especially YouTube) for visibility in AI search surfaces. Third, his approach to AI content production has evolved from manual, single-prompt assistance toward modular, repeatable systems — "skill files" and agent-driven pipelines that encode an entire editorial process end-to-end. Fourth, there's a consistent tension in his thinking between AI-enabled content efficiency and long-term brand/SEO risk: he's bullish on AI as a production tool but skeptical of using it to simply scale content volume without rigor. Finally, he frames the content marketer's career trajectory as moving up the value chain — from drafting text to orchestrating AI systems, interpreting data, and making strategic editorial calls — a thread that connects his earliest 2023 predictions to his current production workflows.

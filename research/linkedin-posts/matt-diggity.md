## 2. Do Backlinks Matter for AI Overviews?

**Post Title / Topic:** AI Overview ranking signals (brand mentions vs. backlinks)
**Date:** 2025
**Post URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/mattdiggityseo_do-backlinks-matter-for-ai-overviews-activity-7346069925133697025-75Af

**Summary:** Citing an Ahrefs study spanning 75,000 brands, Diggity reported that brand web mentions correlate far more strongly with AI Overview visibility (0.664) than backlinks do (0.218) — roughly a threefold difference in signal strength. He frames this as evidence that AI search systems weigh how often and in what context a brand is discussed across the web more heavily than traditional link-based authority. His takeaway is that earning unlinked brand mentions through PR, reviews, and community discussion deserves investment on par with classic link building for AI search visibility.

**Key Insight:** Brand mention frequency and context now outweigh backlink volume as a predictor of AI Overview visibility, signaling a shift in where SEO and digital PR budget should go.

**Relevance to AI SEO Research:** Provides a quantified, data-backed comparison of two competing authority signals specifically for AI Overview visibility, useful for prioritizing AI SEO tactics and reallocating link-building vs. PR investment.

## Overall Themes

Across all five posts, Diggity treats AI's core value in SEO as a speed and cost multiplier that enables scaling — more content, faster topical authority, and faster diagnostic workflows — rather than a guaranteed quality upgrade on its own. A second consistent thread is that AI search surfaces (AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Copilot, Perplexity) follow meaningfully different visibility rules than classic Google SERPs: brand mentions outweigh backlinks, citation cannibalization between pages is a new failure mode, and structural choices like FAQ schema and front-loaded answers directly affect citation rates. Third, the craft of getting good results from AI — prompt engineering, agent workflows, and structured automation — is repeatedly framed as the real differentiator between content or workflows that perform and those that don't, more than the choice of AI tool itself. Fourth, Diggity consistently grounds his claims in data and experiments (Ahrefs and Surfer studies, internal case studies, citation counts) rather than speculation, reflecting an experiment-driven approach to AI SEO. Finally, his content visibly tracks the industry's shift over time: 2022-2024 posts focus on tactical "how to use ChatGPT for SEO tasks," while 2025-2026 posts move toward AI-agent-driven technical workflows and GEO-specific tactics aimed squarely at AI Overviews and LLM answer engines.

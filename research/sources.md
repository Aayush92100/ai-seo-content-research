# Research Sources

**Project:** AI-Powered SEO Content Production for B2B SaaS
**Last Verified:** June 19, 2026

## Executive Summary

These 10 experts were selected because each occupies a distinct, non-overlapping position in the AI-powered SEO content stack — from raw AI content generation, to the semantic/topical frameworks that keep that content discoverable, to the operational systems and distribution motions that turn content into pipeline. Together they span four functional layers: **strategy** (what to build and why), **production** (how content gets made, including AI tooling), **structure** (how content earns authority with search engines and LLMs), and **distribution** (how content reaches buyers once published). This set deliberately includes both software builders (Rankability, Clearscope, Byword) and practitioner-educators (Authority Hacker, Gotch SEO, Goldie Agency), giving the research base both a "what works in production" and a "what the data says" perspective. Two of the ten — Ryan Law (Ahrefs) and Kevin Indig (ex-Shopify/G2/Atlassian) — bring direct, large-scale B2B SaaS operating experience, anchoring the more aggressive affiliate/agency tactics of the others in enterprise reality.

# Research Coverage Analysis

**Strengths of this expert set.** The collection covers the full AI-SEO content lifecycle without redundancy: Koray Tuğberk GÜBÜR anchors the *structural* layer (topical authority, semantic frameworks) that any AI content program needs to avoid producing disconnected, low-authority pages at scale. Matt Diggity, Julian Goldie, and Jake Ward represent the *production* layer — practitioners actively using AI to generate content faster, with Ward's Byword product offering a builder's view of how AI writing tools are engineered. Nathan Gotch and Bernard Huang sit at the *tooling* layer, having built AI-powered optimization software (Rankability, Clearscope) that operationalizes content quality at scale. Kevin Indig and Ryan Law provide the *strategic and enterprise* layer, both having led SEO/content functions inside well-known SaaS companies (Shopify, G2, Atlassian, Ahrefs, Animalz) — they ground the research in real B2B SaaS budgets, org structures, and trade-offs rather than affiliate-marketing economics. Ross Simmonds closes the loop with *distribution*, a layer frequently ignored in "AI content production" research despite being equally critical to ROI. Gael Breton adds a systems/automation lens that bridges production and operations.

**Overlap between experts.** There is intentional, productive overlap rather than redundancy. Kevin Indig, Nathan Gotch, and Ryan Law all address AI-search/LLM visibility, but from different vantage points — Indig from a cross-company strategic advisory lens, Gotch from a tool-builder's optimization lens, and Law from a single-company (Ahrefs) operational lens. This triangulation is valuable: where their conclusions agree, confidence is high; where they diverge, it signals genuine uncertainty in the field worth flagging to leadership. Similarly, Matt Diggity and Julian Goldie both demonstrate AI content production at affiliate/agency scale, but Diggity emphasizes test-based rigor while Goldie emphasizes automation breadth — together they show both the careful and the aggressive ends of the same practice. Bernard Huang and Nathan Gotch both build AI content-optimization software, giving a useful product-comparison angle (Clearscope's NLP scoring vs. Rankability's IBM Watson–based topic extraction).

**Why this set is sufficient for a future AI SEO playbook.** A practical playbook needs four ingredients: a defensible content structure, repeatable AI-assisted production methods, tooling to verify quality at scale, and a distribution motion to convert visibility into pipeline. This set of ten experts maps cleanly onto all four, with at least one high-priority source per layer (Koray for structure; Diggity/Gotch/Goldie for production; Huang/Gotch for tooling; Simmonds for distribution) and two enterprise-grade SaaS operators (Law, Indig) to stress-test tactics against real B2B economics. No further expert additions are required to begin playbook drafting; the next research step should focus on extracting specific, citable frameworks and quotes from each source (a `research/frameworks.md` deliverable) rather than expanding the source list further.
